The inventor of the improved light switch

John Henry Holmes created the new and improved design for the light switch which is still used to some extent today. He was the inventor of the "quick-break technology" that prevented electrical arcing when turning the light on and off, drastically increasing the longevity of both the switch and the bulb.

The abomination that is automated electronic switches

Recent "developments" have resulted in the creation of a variety of automated systems that dare to replace the usage of traditional light switches. Take for example, motion sensor switches. For starters, if you hold still in a room for too long, you have to shimmy around until the lights come back on. A drastic inconvenience. Furthermore, the remove any sense of punctuation that accompanies entering and exiting a room with the flick of a light switch. Worse yet are auditory light switches, which turn the lights on and off at the clap of your hands. This is outrageous. Demanding that a person clap their hands loud enough that the installed microphones can hear it can potentially cause palm injuries, one of the very same injuries light switches are put in place to avoid.
